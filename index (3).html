A full life, a dream wedding,
and one big question.

"I'm a small-town girl from West Tennessee who has never once let a full calendar get in the way of what matters — and right now, what matters most is that I'm planning my wedding."

Between a demanding traveling career, a seat on a nonprofit board, a horse who absolutely deserves more trail rides, two opinionated ferrets, and a border collie with boundless energy, my life doesn't leave much room for chaos. And then, at 33, the greatest thing happened: Kyler proposed.

I have been dreaming about this wedding since I was a teenager — the feeling of it, the details, the vision that has lived in my head for years. And now that it's actually happening, now that I'm deep in the middle of planning a dream wedding in Colorado while keeping everything else in motion, I want this season to feel the way it was always supposed to feel: clear, grounded, and genuinely joyful.

The first reality check was what weddings actually cost — and the second was everything that comes with planning one. Vendors, timelines, contracts, seating charts, guest lists, tastings, fittings, and a hundred details that all need to live somewhere that isn't three different browser tabs and a notes app at two in the morning.

I have a wonderful wedding planner — she is genuinely amazing — but even with her guidance, everything still ends up in spreadsheet after spreadsheet, and the mental load of keeping it all connected falls squarely on me. I started looking at every app I could find, and what I discovered was that most of them are built to serve the market, not the bride.

What I was looking for didn't seem to exist: something genuinely on my side, that could hold all the logistics without losing sight of the fact that there is a person behind them who is allowed to be excited, emotional, and completely human about all of this. So I built it.

ClearBride is the tool I built while planning my own wedding — which means every feature, every calm check-in, every gentle reminder came from a real moment when I needed it too. I'm still using it. Still planning. Still figuring it out right alongside you. That's what "we're in this together" actually means.

— Brittany ✦
